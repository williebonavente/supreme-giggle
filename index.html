Philippine Holidays

A comprehensive list of public holidays in the Philippines.

New Year's Day

January 1

A celebration marking the beginning of the new year.

Day of Valor

April 9

The Day of Valor honors Filipino and American soldiers who bravely fought during World War II, particularly the Bataan Death March, highlighting their sacrifices and heroism.

Labor Day

May 1

Labor Day recognizes the contributions of Filipino workers and promotes workers' rights.

Independence's Day

June 12

Independence Day marks the Philippines' declaration of independence from Spanish rule in 1898, featuring parades, flag-raising ceremonies, and cultural activities that showcase national pride.

All Saint's day

November 1

All Saints' Day honors all saints and deceased loved ones, with Filipinos visiting cemeteries to offer prayers, light candles, and share meals.

Bonifacio Day

November 30

Bonifacio Day commemorates the birth of Andrés Bonifacio, a national hero and leader of the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule, symbolizing the fight for freedom and independence.

Christmas Day

December 25

Christmas Day in the Philippines is a joyful occasion marked by family gatherings, festive meals, and vibrant traditions, reflecting the country’s strong Christian faith.

Rizal Day

December 30

A celebration marking the beginning of the new year.Observed on December 30, Rizal Day commemorates the life and legacy of Dr. José Rizal, a national hero.
